Understanding Public Lands

Not all protected areas are the same. Here is a quick breakdown of the differences between federal designations.

Designation Primary Purpose Management
National Park Preserve scenic beauty, wildlife, and natural heritage. generally large and diverse. National Park Service (DOI)
National Monument Preserve specific natural, cultural, or scientific interest. Can be a single object/site. NPS, BLM, or Forest Service
National Forest Multiple use: recreation, timber, grazing, wildlife, and water conservation. US Forest Service (USDA)

Spotlight: The Return of the Wolf

One of the greatest conservation success stories in North American history is the reintroduction of the gray wolf to Yellowstone National Park. After being eradicated in the 1920s, wolves were brought back in 1995.

This reintroduction triggered a "trophic cascade," regulating elk populations and allowing willow and aspen trees to recover, which in turn brought back beavers and songbirds.

Yosemite: A First Timer's Guide

Category: National Parks

Yosemite National Park is a shrine to human foresight. First protected in 1864, it is best known for its waterfalls, but within its nearly 1,200 square miles, you can find deep valleys, grand meadows, ancient giant sequoias, a vast wilderness area, and much more. Plan your visit during the shoulder seasons (May/September) to avoid the biggest crowds at Tunnel View.

Understanding Wildlife Distances

Category: Wildlife

Seeing a bear or bison is a highlight of any trip, but safety is paramount. The general rule is 100 yards (a football field) for bears and wolves, and 25 yards for bison and elk. Never feed wildlife; it habituates them to humans and often leads to the animal's death. Learn how to use a telephoto lens effectively to get great shots safely.
